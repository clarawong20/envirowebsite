Problem with Laundry

The clothing we wear, especially if bought from chain stores, contains many plastic fibres and chemicals. When putting our clothes through the wash, tiny microfibres and microplastics detach and end up in the waterways since they are often too small to be caught by water filtration systems. These microfibres then flow into waterways and ecosystems. Laundry detergent itself also contributes to plastic waste since it often comes in excessive plastic packaging that is difficult to repurpose after it fulfills its purpose. On top of contamination issues, laundry machines also use more water and energy as compared to hand washing and drying.
