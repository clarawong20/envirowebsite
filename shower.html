Problem with Shower heads

Typically 20% of every shower in duration is lost. For a standard showerhead, every minute wasted equates to around 2.5 gallons of water used. Research conducted by Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory has suggested that the waste levels may be even higher. Around 30 % of shower water overall and 41 % of hot water energy is wasted. Showers are typically one of the largest drags on the hot water heater in a home and on electricity usage as a whole. Cutting back on hot water waste in showers has the benefit of saving water and power, as well as associated costs on both water and power bills.
