Problem with Technology

E-waste or electronic waste is comprised of electronics that are unwanted or not working. Due to the increasing speed at which technology and technological demands are increasing, so is the amount of e-waste. There are many types of e-waste. From batteries to cellphones to speakers to record players. This waste is often incredibly dangerous to discard due to toxic chemicals such as mercury, cadmium and lead. If not disposed of properly, they can pose an environmental risk for the surrounding soil, water, air, and wildlife. Even when disposed into landfills, these chemicals eventually begin to leak into the soil and groundwater below, contaminating freshwater sources. Mining for these new metals required for technology requires heavy machinery releasing greenhouse gases and also exposing workers to dangerous fumes and safety hazards. 78% of e-waste ends up in landfills instead of being recycled. 30% of electronic material cannot be recovered, even when it is placed in the recycling. With more than 44 million tons of e-waste being produced per year, recycling and repurposing old electronics is crucial to increasing sustainability.
