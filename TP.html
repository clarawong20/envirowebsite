Problem with Toilet Paper

According to the NRDC, the industry clear-cuts around one million acres of boreal forest each year. Canada is ranked third globally, behind Russia and Brazil, in terms of global intact forest loss. Much of this is in order to produce a pulp that U.S. tissue makers roll into toilet paper. This contributes to the continued deforestation and subsequent decrease in carbon storage. Not only are animals dependent on these boreal forests for survival, but deforestation is greatly affecting more than 600 Indigenous communities. These communities depend on the forests for food, shelter, cultural and spiritual activities.
