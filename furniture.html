Problem with Furniture

The fast furniture industry is characterized by the use of toxic substances and unsustainable materials. By continuously buying and then throwing out furniture every home renovation or with every move, furniture waste can be estimated to be around 12 million tons per year. This has a disproportionately larger ecological footprint due to the vast resources necessary to make furniture as compared to other household items. For example, metal appliances or wooden furniture require not just their composite materials, but also water and electricity to build them.

Solutions:

Cheap:

If your furniture is unsalvageable or difficult to renovate, antique stores and second-hand stores are a good place to start looking for new furniture. The furniture there is far cheaper than that found in stores and by reusing old furniture, you are limiting the production of new furniture and decreasing your ecological footprint.

Brands:

If you want furniture that is more modern and you can’t find any at secondhand or antique stores, shop sustainable brands! Brands that have transparency policies and source their materials sustainably reduce their own ecological footprint beforehand as compared to major brands like IKEA or the Brick.
